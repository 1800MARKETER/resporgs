### Toll-Free Messaging Verification Rule Effective January 2026

The toll-free messaging verification rule, mandating Business Registration Number (BRN), issuing country, and legal entity type in submissions, stems from **industry-wide carrier requirements** implemented by toll-free service providers and carriers (e.g., via Somos-managed processes), not a direct FCC mandate, though aligned with FCC anti-robocall efforts.[2][3][4][5][6] It takes effect for new verifications starting **January 1, 2026** (with some carriers enforcing by February 17, 2026), as part of aligning toll-free A2P messaging with 10DLC standards to combat spam.[1][2][3][4]

#### 1. Issuer of the Rule
Industry carriers and toll-free administrators (e.g., Somos ecosystem) are driving the change through updated verification forms, described as "industry-wide" and "carrier requirements" to enhance trust and reduce fraud, without explicit FCC rulemaking cited.[1][3][4][5][6] No primary FCC docket or Federal Register entry directly mandates it in results; it's positioned as voluntary alignment with FCC goals like robocall mitigation.[2][9]

#### 2. Affected Submissions
Only **new toll-free verifications** (net-new Toll-Free Verified Sender Forms or A2P registrations) require BRN details; existing verified numbers and renewals are exempt—no resubmission needed.[3][4]

#### 3. Valid BRN Examples
- **US entities**: Primarily **EIN** (Employer Identification Number) from IRS; sole proprietors without EIN select "Sole Proprietor" entity type (US-only, triggers manual review, not guaranteed).[4][2]
- **Non-US entities**: Official registration number from local/national authority (e.g., Companies House UK, Corporations Canada/CRA, or equivalent); must include legal name, issuing authority, and ISO 3166-1 Alpha-2 country code (e.g., CA, GB).[4][6]
- Other mentions: DUNS possible but not primary; state licenses not specified as valid standalone.[4][7] Fields: BRN, Registration Type (e.g., EIN/CRA), Country code, Entity Type (e.g., for-profit, non-profit).[1][4][6]

#### 4. Non-Compliance Penalties
Submissions **may be rejected** without BRN; potential **messaging traffic block/suspension** if verification fails, though some providers note solutions for lacking BRN to avoid full service removal.[2][3][5] Processing may delay for manual sole proprietor reviews.[4]

#### 5. Timeline and Grace Period

#### 6. Relation to 10DLC Vetting
